Blue Ridge Parkway Travel Information

Overview:
The Blue Ridge Parkway is a scenic highway connecting Shenandoah National Park in
Virginia with Great Smoky Mountains National Park in North Carolina and Tennessee.
It is often called "America's Favorite Drive" because of its mountain views and
natural scenery.

Location Near Asheville:
The Blue Ridge Parkway is easily accessible from Asheville.
Many visitors use Asheville as a base to explore sections of the parkway.
Popular areas near Asheville include Craggy Gardens, Mount Pisgah, and Graveyard Fields.

Activities:
The Blue Ridge Parkway offers many outdoor activities:

- Scenic driving with mountain and valley views.
- Hiking on trails ranging from easy walks to challenging mountain hikes.
- Photography at overlooks and scenic viewpoints.
- Wildlife viewing, especially birds and native mountain animals.
- Picnicking at designated areas.

Popular Hiking Areas:

Craggy Gardens:
- Located north of Asheville.
- Known for rhododendron blooms in late spring and early summer.
- Offers short hikes and panoramic mountain views.

Mount Pisgah:
- Located south of Asheville.
- Features a popular summit hike.
- The summit provides wide views of surrounding mountains.

Graveyard Fields:
- Located near milepost 418.
- Known for waterfalls, open fields, and hiking trails.
- Popular trails include access to Upper Falls and Second Falls.

Planning Tips:
- The parkway has limited services, so visitors should bring water, snacks, and
  necessary hiking supplies.
- Weather conditions can change quickly at higher elevations.
- Some sections may close temporarily due to weather or road maintenance.
- Sunrise and sunset are popular times for scenic views.

Recommended Trip Length:
A Blue Ridge Parkway visit can range from a few hours for scenic driving to a
full day for hiking and exploring multiple viewpoints.
